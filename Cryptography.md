Core principles of cryptography

Cryptography is built on four core principles. Together, these principles form a comprehensive framework that secures the complex landscape of digital communications, data storage, and transaction processing. Collectively referred to as CIA+N, adhering to these principles ensures that the data is stored, transmitted, and accessed as securely as possible. The four principles of the CIA+N framework are explained below:

    Confidentiality ensures that unauthorized users cannot access information, protecting sensitive data from unauthorized access.

    Integrity maintains data accuracy and consistency, safeguarding it from unauthorized alterations.

    Authentication confirms the identities of entities involved in communication, establishing trust in the legitimacy of participants.

    Non-repudiation prevents parties from denying their actions, ensuring accountability in digital interactions.
